## Purpose of Discovery

The Discovery phase aims to build a **shared understanding of the business problem, domain, scope, and constraints** before moving into solution design and implementation.

For the ISBUSHKA project, Discovery focuses on:
- understanding salon operations and pain points,
- defining business goals and success criteria,
- aligning stakeholders and user roles,
- establishing domain language and boundaries,
- fixing scope, assumptions, and risks early,
- preparing a solid foundation for further design and delivery activities.

This phase deliberately avoids technical implementation details and UI design.

---

## Discovery Outcomes

As a result of the Discovery phase, the following outcomes were achieved:

- Clear business vision and objectives
- Documented current-state problems (As-Is)
- Identified stakeholders, actors, and user goals
- Defined system scope and explicit exclusions
- Established domain model and terminology
- Captured high-level functional expectations and business rules
- Identified key risks, assumptions, constraints, and dependencies
- Clarified data sources and ownership

Discovery is considered **completed and approved** once these artifacts are in place.

## Transition to Next Phase

With Discovery completed, the project transitions into the **Design phase**, starting with:

- Functional Requirements detailing
- Functional Specifications
- Business process modeling (BPMN To-Be)
- Architecture and system design artifacts

Discovery artifacts serve as the **baseline and source of truth** for all subsequent phases.
